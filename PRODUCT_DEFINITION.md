# IBUILTTHIS — Product Definition

*Last updated: 2026-03-31 | Status: Early product ideation, pre-technical*

---

## The Why

We are living through a vibe coding wave. Product managers, designers, developers, and other people close to AI and agents are — for the first time ever — building their own products from scratch. Sometimes for themselves. Sometimes they launch it. For many, this is the first time they have shipped something they fully owned.

This creates a new need: **a place to exist as a builder.** People want to show off what they've done, prove they know what they know, and point others (peers, bosses, recruiters, other builders) to something tangible. That place does not exist yet in the right form for this audience.

**IBUILTTHIS wants to be that place.**

---

## For Whom

**Primary user: Product managers** who are building products as they explore the world of AI and agentic AI. They are tinkering, learning, and shipping — often for the first time. They want to demonstrate that they can actually build, not just talk about building.

**Secondary user: Designers** doing exactly the same thing, just with a designer lens. Tinkering, prototyping, shipping with AI.

Both audiences share the same core motivation: to show their skill, their journey, and the fact that they built something real.

---

## For What (Core Jobs to Be Done)

1. **"I want to show off what I built and the skill it took"** — credibility, pride, professional signaling
2. **"I want to follow builders I admire and see their process"** — community, inspiration
3. **"I want to understand where to start so I can build something myself"** — the biggest unsolved pain point: too many tools, don't know what to type, don't know where to begin

---

## Discovery & Social Layer

**Primary audience for discovery: other vibe coders.** Not recruiters (yet). Peers following the story — what someone built, what led to it, what tools and skills they used, how they iterated.

The platform is socially shareable. A project card should be something you can drop in Slack, share with a peer, send to your boss.

**HR/recruiting pivot is possible** (showing you can build with AI is increasingly a career signal), but this is not the focus now. It's a future option if the community signals it.

---

## The Core Object: The Project Card

The central unit of the platform is a **project card** — not a build log (too verbose), not just a link (too thin). Think of it as a slide-deck summary of someone's build journey:

- What the product is and does
- What worked
- What failed / what they tried
- What tools and skills were used
- How it iterated over time
- Where it stands today

**Critical principle: the card is generated, not manually created.** The builder should not feel like they're doing admin work. The platform captures the story for them. This eliminates the biggest enemy of portfolios: friction of maintaining them.

## The User Journey (Envisioned)

The platform is best understood as a **vibe coder's home base** — not just a portfolio destination, but a place where the journey starts and is tracked.

### Step 1 — Discovery / Entry

Someone wants to build something but doesn't know where to start. They discover IBUILTTHIS.

### Step 2 — Kickstart

The platform helps them get going:

- Guidance on which tools exist and what each is good for (a real gap: the ecosystem is overwhelming and there is no good "if you want X, use Y, but when you hit Z switch to W" guide)
- A first prompt or idea to start building
- Resources: examples from other builders, YouTube-style walkthroughs, idea exploration exercises (e.g. "play out your idea like a PM before you start coding")
- Orientation: where you'll hit walls with each tool, how to migrate a project using GitHub as a bridge, etc.

### Step 3 — Building

The builder is off building. The platform follows along as they go — capturing tools used, decisions made, iterations.

### Step 4 — Showcase

A project card is automatically generated and tells the story. The builder can share it, embed it, point people to it.

---

## Key Differentiators

- **Journey > destination.** The "how I got here" is as important as the product itself. Other platforms (Product Hunt, GitHub) only show the output.
- **Generated, not filled in.** No manual portfolio maintenance. The card builds itself.
- **Starts at the beginning.** The platform isn't just where you end up — it's where you start. That gives it full-journey data and a reason to exist before you've shipped anything.
- **Tool compass.** A practical guide to the overwhelming vibe coding tool landscape — nobody has built this well yet.

## Open Questions (To Revisit)

1. **One product or two?** There is a tension between two modes:
  - A **resource/guide** for people starting out (tool compass, kickstart prompts, idea exploration)
  - A **showcase platform** for publishing what you've built
   Both are valuable, both serve the same audience, but they have different core loops. Is this one integrated product, or is one the core and the other supporting? **This was the last open question when we stopped.**
2. What does "the platform follows along" actually mean technically? (Deferred — product first.)
3. How much of the kickstart is content (curated resources) vs. interactive (the platform actively guides you)?
